When honey is "filtered," it has been processed to eliminate tiny particles, frequently ones that are even smaller than pollen. The process of straining honey, which is frequently used to remove bee parts and other big debris before canning while keeping the pollen and other valuable components of honey, differs from filtering honey. When honey is filtered, it is frequently heated to make it more liquid in order to facilitate filtering, rendering the honey no longer raw.
